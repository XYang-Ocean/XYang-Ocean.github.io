Ocean biogeochemistry · Southern Ocean · BGC-Argo

Understanding how ocean physics, sea ice, and biology shape carbon cycling in the Southern Ocean.

I am an ocean biogeochemist working at the interface of physical oceanography, autonomous observations, and carbon-cycle research. My work uses BGC-Argo floats, shipboard measurements, sediment traps, and time-series observations to study air–sea CO₂ exchange, carbon export, Antarctic Winter Water, and sea-ice-linked ecosystem variability.

About Me

I am currently a postdoctoral researcher in the GATO Lab at Georgia Tech. My postdoctoral project focuses on Antarctic Winter Water contribution to carbon exchange across the Southern Ocean and its interaction with sea-ice variability.

Before moving to Atlanta, I completed my PhD in Quantitative Marine Science at the Institute for Marine and Antarctic Studies, University of Tasmania, where I studied drivers of changes in air–sea CO₂ exchange and carbon cycling in the Subantarctic and coastal Antarctica.
